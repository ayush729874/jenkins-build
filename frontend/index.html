FAQ
Frequently Asked
Questions
What is a URL shortener?
A URL shortener converts a long web address into a compact, shareable link. For example, a 200-character Google Docs URL becomes something like treecom.site/aB3xK9z. When someone clicks the short link, they are instantly redirected to the original long URL. It's useful for social media bios, text messages, printed materials, or any situation where a long URL would look messy.
Is Treecom really free? Are there any hidden charges?
Yes, completely free — no fine print. Treecom does not have a paid plan, a premium tier, or any feature gating. You can shorten as many links as you want without ever paying a rupee (or any currency). There are no ads shown to people who click your links either.
Do I need to create an account?
No. Treecom is designed to be as frictionless as possible. Just open the website, paste your URL, and click Shorten. No email address, no password, no verification — nothing. Your short link is ready in under a second.
How does Treecom generate short links?
When you submit a URL, our FastAPI backend stores it in a MySQL database and assigns a unique integer ID. That ID is then encoded using Base62 (characters 0–9, a–z, A–Z), producing a short 7-character code. This approach creates over 3.5 trillion unique possible codes — so you'll never run out.
Are my links safe? Does Treecom track clicks?
Treecom does not inject tracking scripts, sell data, or profile visitors. Links redirect cleanly with no intermediary ad page. We do not share your original URLs with any third party. The service is privacy-respecting by design.
Do short links expire?
Links generated on Treecom do not have a built-in expiry. They remain active as long as the service is running. There is no time limit on how long your short links stay valid.
